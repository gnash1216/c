Sugar Conundrum

Sugar or more specifically, refined sugar or white sugar has always been there to bring comfort and pleasure to our taste buds. Its sweetness is like no other. And it creates an insatiable feeling of wanting for more. That is perhaps the reason why we just can’t keep away from it. Well, if it is that good then why is it considered bad for our health? To try to understand this question and even try to answer it, let us first categorize how we refer to it. 

By its physical property, “white sugar” or refined sugar” is a substance commonly in crystalline or powder form that when added to drinks or food acts as a sweetener. It makes our drink or food to taste… sweet. So it makes food more agreeable or palatable. It is derived from sugar cane through a process of refinement. On the other hand, it also carries its chemical property. By this, we mean that sugar, no matter what name, form, shape, or origin, is… sugar.

So sugar, technically, upon consumption, makes our brain release a chemical known as dopamine. This gives us that “feel-good” feeling that makes us want to have more sugar. Refined sugar makes our brain release huge amounts of this dopamine. That is why most people would seemingly have an insatiable craving for soda, cakes, and especially candies. All because of the added sugar in it. Fruits would naturally have sugar in it but do not cause our brain to release as much dopamine as “sugar” does. Whenever we “feel good” it also gives us a burst of energy that affects our mood positively.

On the downside, a lot of studies have shown that sugar affects and negatively impacts our health. Such studies would show that it causes problems to our heart, kidney and, other vital parts and function of our body. All these are brought about by the reaction of sugar to other chemicals in our bodies. We will not dwell on the details of these chemical reactions anymore.

Having said that, while many experts say that, “sugar is bad” is kind of vague. It can’t be true, right? Afterall sugar is present in whole, natural food we eat. So what is exactly bad about sugar is the excessive amount that we put in our bodies. So it is the act of taking too much of it that makes it “bad”. Nevertheless, it is this notion of sugar being bad that creates an opportunity to find alternatives to sugar as we know it.
